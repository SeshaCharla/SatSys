\subsection{Distribution of the Parameter Estimate}
Using asymptotic properties of MLE (Theorem-7.3 in \cite{kay1993estimation}), with significantly large data length $(N)$, the parameter estimate $\hat \theta_{sat}$ asymptotically follows a normal distribution under some regularity conditions on the PDF $p(\pmb \varepsilon_n; \theta_{sat})$.
\begin{align}
        \hat \theta_{sat} \sim \mathcal{N} \lr{\theta_{sat},  I^{-1}(\theta_{sat})}
\end{align}
where, $I(\theta_{sat})$ is the fisher information matrix evaluated at the true value of the parameter $(\theta_{sat})$. Here, $I$ is evaluated at the MLE estimate. We have the $(i,j)^{th}$ element of the fisher information matrix:
\begin{align}
        \lrb{I(\theta_{sat})}_{ij} &= -E \lrb{\frac{\partial^2 \ln p(\varepsilon_\eta; \theta_{sat})}{\partial \theta_j \partial \theta_i}}
\end{align}
The regularity conditions require the existence of derivatives of the log-likelihood function and the Fisher information being non-zero.
